About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Finance at Florida International University. My research focuses on asset pricing, hedge funds, market microstructure, option pricing, and FinTech. I am particularly interested in higher-moment asset pricing, skewness-seeking behavior, and data-driven methods in finance.

Research

Resolving the Higher-Moment Pricing Puzzle: Systematic and Idiosyncratic Risk Across Penny and Non-Penny Stocks

with Mustafa Caglayan and Diogo Duarte

We examine whether variance, skewness, and kurtosis predict expected returns once decomposed into systematic and idiosyncratic components. Using U.S. common stocks from 1963–2024, we show that the mixed evidence in the literature reflects pooling two distinct markets. Splitting by nominal price resolves the higher-moment pricing puzzle: penny stocks earn negative premia for all three moments, whereas non-penny stocks earn positive premia, strongest for idiosyncratic skewness and kurtosis.

Who Seeks Skewness? Payoff Engineering in Hedge Funds

We examine whether hedge funds deliberately seek positively skewed, lottery-like payoffs and assess the economic implications of such behavior. Using a comprehensive panel of hedge funds from 1994 to 2024, we show that positive skewness is rare but persistent. Funds with persistently high skewness attract significantly higher investor flows and earn superior returns, while not exhibiting higher exit risk.
