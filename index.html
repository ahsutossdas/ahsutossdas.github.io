01Working Paper

Resolving the Higher-Moment Pricing Puzzle: Systematic and Idiosyncratic Risk Across Penny and Non-Penny Stocks

Ahsutoss Das, Mustafa Caglayan, and Diogo Duarte

Analyzes U.S. equity markets (1963–2024) by decomposing higher-moment risks into systematic and idiosyncratic components. The study demonstrates that conflicting findings in prior literature arise from pooling structurally different market segments. Results provide new evidence on risk compensation and investor demand for tail-risk exposures in U.S. capital markets.

02Working Paper

Who Seeks Skewness? Payoff Engineering in Hedge Funds

Ahsutoss Das

Examines whether hedge fund managers actively design positively skewed payoff structures. Using a comprehensive hedge fund panel (1994–2024), the study shows that skewness-seeking behavior attracts investor capital and improves performance without increasing failure risk, highlighting investor demand for convex payoff profiles in alternative investments.
